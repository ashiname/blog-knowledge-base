The week was crap and I can't multitask

And again I’m passing through the same experience I’ve run through some months ago. It’s way too hard for me to multitask.

May to June this year (2025) I worked at a place and it was such an excruciating experience that I simply stopped doing anything else I liked, even in my free time from work.

Then I was unemployed again, and only then I realized how I basically died for my own life on that 3 months period. All the time I was at that job, I didn't do the things I enjoyed, and I couldn't pay attetion to things I wanted to do next

Been laid off of that job I was unemployd for about a week or so, then I got another job, but this time it was a part time job at a store. So for the short period I was in there (1,5 month) this kind of didn't happened again.

As it was a position at a store, whenever there wasn't any customers I was free to listen to music, read, or anything else.

More recently, the current time I'm writing this, again I started working on another full time job, and as I was experiencing that feeling of abandoning myself, and being experienced in the matter, it made me do the active experiment that was saying like: "— Okay now I'm gonna stop what I'm doing a little and Imma try to think about my own life.". But kind of... I don't know how to explain but I simply couldn't.

I'm in kind of a leading position, so this problem seems to become even worse because I have to pay attention in how other people are working, so I don't know if I get kind of worried by how the things are working out, but when I tried to sit down a little and grab my phone to write some diary or analyze something I had to do next, my head was just... empty. It's as a bizarre feeling that is very confusing to explain.

As I explained before in a previous post, I hate to "go hiatus". So because of that, and realizing I cannot work full time while paying attention that I still exist and want to do things beside of working, I created a Google Sheets specifically to put things I watched, listened to and read to remember when was the last time I did those things I liked. But EVEN that way I AM STILL NOT ABLE to keep up with things I like the same time I get a "full working week".

To me it's very obvious it would work, it accounts the last time I read, watched a movie or listened to an album, three things I like to do. But even having this active reminder... I can't.

Worst thing is I CAN see the reason.

While at work site most of the times I can't stop and think about other stuff outside from it, but when I come home I have free time, but I waste it. I watch Youtube videos I don't even like anymore or care, but I'm too used to, and scroll Twitter, and by doing that... the time just flies by right in front of my face.

Life is like this. When you least expect it's been an entire year and you didn't even noticed... Even setting up a lot of reminders I couldn't yet ease this feeling. This has been the most significant feeling when it comes to review that I didn't do something I was meaning to. "The times just flew by, I don't- I- I don't know what happened, the time just flies and I couldn't do it" it's the exact 100% way of how this is addressed in my head

I'm tired of this feeling. At work I cannot think, while I'm using my computer I cannot think too because of too many distractions.

That working is this bullshit where you lose the rest of your entire life I think everybody who ever worked already knew. But I think I may be possibly making it even worse, maybe because I don't make enough effort to take my mind off of work a little, or because when I have the free time to to the said things I waste it with other time consuming bullshit.

And at the end of the day I don't even know how to conclude this text. Normally I would finish with kind of anticipating the next episodes, but I don't know... I do hope this can change, but I needed to be an active voice in my own life, I needed to take part at every hour and do everything I do with intention, and I do want to do it, that was exactly the reason I created a blog...

Let's hope that writing things and the other things I bothered to write and express here can be a spark that says I AM able to change my life for better.
